During my junior year, I worked on two separate robots: one built for durability and competition, and another streamlined for skill-based autonomous runs.

We leaned into the idea that sometimes a collision could be a calculated calibration — intentionally hitting known surfaces allowed us to localize without relying on perfect tuning.
